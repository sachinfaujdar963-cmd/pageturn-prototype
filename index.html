Chapter 1 · The Library
The Midnight Library
Chapter One

Somewhere out beyond the edge of the universe there is a library that contains an infinite number of books, each one the story of another reality. One tells the story of your life as it is, along with another book for the other life you could have lived if you had made a different choice at any point in your life.

The books were all identical in size and shape, bound in dark green leather with gold lettering on their spines. The only difference was the titles.

Nora Seed had always found it hard to be here, in this life. She had been told she was too sensitive, too easily hurt, too quick to take things personally. She had spent years trying to build up a shell around herself, but it never quite hardened the way she wanted it to.

On the evening of the nineteenth of October, at approximately eleven twenty-two, Nora decided she'd had enough. She was thirty-five years old. She had failed at most things she had tried — including her marriage, her relationship with her family, her attempt to become an Olympic swimmer, and her career as a musician.

But between life and death there is a library, and within that library, the shelves go on forever. Every book provides a chance to try another life you could have lived. To see how things would be different if you had made other choices. Would you have been happier if you'd chosen differently?

She opened the door. The Midnight Library, it seemed, was not much of a library at all, in the physical sense of the word. The room was empty except for a wooden desk in the center, behind which sat a woman who looked remarkably like Nora's school librarian, Mrs. Elm.
